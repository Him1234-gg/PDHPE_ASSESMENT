Common hurdles

The challenges ahead

The Y6–Y7 transition is one of the most significant shifts in a student's school life. Understanding what's coming makes it much easier to prepare.

01

Transitioning between classrooms

In year 6, students mostly stay in one classroom. However in year 7, students have to move around the school through out the day.

02

Assessment

In primary school, teats are rare, but in high school, you may have multiple assessment tasks in the same week.

03

Bigger environment

Secondary schools are physically larger with more students. Navigating timetables, buildings, and routines can feel disorienting in the early weeks.

Strategies that work

Eight evidence-informed approaches to help students land confidently and thrive from day one in Year 7.

🤝

Build your friendship network early

Attend orientation events, join a club or team in the first half term, and say yes to invitations. Early connections become your anchor when things feel hard.

Social
📅

Always have a revision timetable

By having a revision timetable that you follow its going to be easier to keep track of all of your work and assingments.

Your transition roadmap

What to focus on, and when, across the transition window.

🌱
Term 6 (Y6)

Prepare

Attend open days, talk about feelings, organise stationery and uniform, visit the new school.

🚀
September

Launch

Focus on names, routines and the planner. Don't worry about grades yet — just settle in.

🌿
Oct–Dec

Root

Join a club, establish study habits, ask for help early. Friendships deepen this half term.

⭐
Spring term

Thrive

By now, you belong. Set academic goals, take on challenges, and enjoy the new environment.

Mind & body

Looking after the whole student

Academic strategies alone aren't enough. Emotional wellbeing is the foundation that everything else is built on during the transition period.

🧘

5 minutes of calm each morning. A short breathing exercise or quiet breakfast before school sets a steadier tone for the day ahead.

📵

Screens off one hour before bed. Blue light disrupts melatonin production — a phone-free bedtime routine dramatically improves sleep quality.

🏃

Move your body every day. Exercise — even a 20 minute walk — reduces anxiety hormones and boosts focus and mood for hours afterwards.

🗣️

Weekly family check-ins. A regular, relaxed conversation about school — not grades — keeps communication channels open before problems escalate.
